What is Genetic Engineering?

Genetic engineering is the field relating to the altering of an organisms genetic code by inserting genes from a foreign source. There are many ways to go about this process and a few will be explained throughout this website. It is a relatively new field with a promising future with advancements in medicine, agriculture, breeding, and many more.

Is Genetic Engineering Dangerous?

The potential for catastrophy when performing genetic engineering practices is somewhat high, depending on what it is you are engineering. In the "History" section of this site, an experiment is outlined where Paul Berg was planning on combining the DNA of a virus and a bacteria that could have created a "super" bacteria that could have started an epidemic. There is also the fear of eventually being able to create "designer babies" where parents will have the ability to pick and choose every attribute that their baby would have. On the other hand, genetic disorders could be a thing of the past by identifying diseases early on in pregnancy and replacing potentially harmful genes with healthy ones. Also, farmers will be able to produce higher yields and healthier foods. In conclusion, genetic engineering practices should be highly regulated so that a catastrophy can never happen.
